# Finance Management Glossary — Macquarie Group
# Domain: Finance, Tax, Treasury
# Maintained by: Finance Architecture, Group Finance

---

## Finance Domain

**Actuals** — Confirmed financial results recorded in the general ledger for a completed period, as opposed to budget or forecast figures.

**Allocation** — The process of distributing shared costs or revenues across business units, cost centres, or legal entities using defined allocation keys (headcount, revenue, floor space, etc.).

**Chart of Accounts (CoA)** — The hierarchical structure of general ledger accounts used to classify financial transactions. Macquarie maintains a Group CoA with entity-level extensions.

**Close Cycle** — The monthly, quarterly, and annual sequence of financial period-end activities: journal posting cutoff, intercompany reconciliation, allocations, consolidation, and reporting sign-off.

**Consolidation** — The process of combining financial statements from multiple legal entities into a single Group view, eliminating intercompany balances and transactions.

**Cost Centre** — An organisational unit used to accumulate costs for management reporting purposes. Macquarie cost centres align to business group and function hierarchies.

**Entity** — A legal entity within the Macquarie Group corporate structure. Entities have their own statutory reporting obligations and may be subject to different regulatory regimes.

**Financial Period** — The accounting period for which financials are reported. Macquarie's financial year ends 31 March; reporting periods are monthly.

**FP&A (Financial Planning & Analysis)** — The function responsible for budgeting, forecasting, variance analysis, and management reporting.

**General Ledger (GL)** — The master record of all financial transactions. Macquarie's primary GL is hosted in SAP S/4HANA.

**Intercompany (IC)** — Transactions between legal entities within the Macquarie Group. All IC transactions must be matched and eliminated at consolidation.

**Journal Entry** — A debit/credit pair posted to the GL to record a financial transaction or adjustment. Journals above a threshold require dual authorisation.

**Legal Entity Controller (LEC)** — The finance professional responsible for the statutory accounts and regulatory submissions of one or more legal entities.

**Management Accounts** — Internal financial reports produced for business management, typically more granular and faster to produce than statutory accounts.

**P&L (Profit and Loss)** — The income statement showing revenues, expenses, and net profit for a period.

**Reconciliation** — The process of matching balances or transactions between two systems or ledgers to confirm agreement or identify discrepancies.

**Restatement** — A correction of previously published financial statements, typically due to material error or accounting policy change.

**Sub-ledger** — A detailed subsidiary ledger that feeds into the GL. Examples: accounts payable sub-ledger, fixed assets register.

**Trial Balance** — A report listing all GL account balances at a point in time, used to verify that debits equal credits before producing financial statements.

**Variance** — The difference between actual results and budget or forecast. Variances are classified as favourable (F) or adverse (A).

## Tax Domain

**APRA Levy** — Annual supervisory levies charged by APRA to regulated entities, accounted for as a period expense.

**ATO (Australian Taxation Office)** — The primary Australian tax authority. Macquarie Group entities registered in Australia file corporate income tax, GST, FBT, and other obligations with the ATO.

**CbCR (Country-by-Country Reporting)** — OECD BEPS Action 13 obligation requiring large multinationals to report revenues, profits, taxes paid, and employee counts for each jurisdiction. Macquarie files CbCR annually with the ATO.

**CFC (Controlled Foreign Corporation)** — An offshore entity in which Macquarie holds a controlling interest. CFC income may be attributed to the Australian parent under Australian tax law.

**CRS (Common Reporting Standard)** — OECD automatic exchange of financial account information standard. Macquarie financial institutions report account holder information to relevant tax authorities.

**DTA / DTL (Deferred Tax Asset / Liability)** — Temporary differences between accounting profit and taxable profit that give rise to future tax adjustments. Recognised on the balance sheet under AASB 112.

**Effective Tax Rate (ETR)** — Total income tax expense divided by pre-tax accounting profit, expressed as a percentage. A key metric for investor reporting.

**FATCA (Foreign Account Tax Compliance Act)** — US legislation requiring financial institutions to report US persons' account information to the IRS. Macquarie holds a Global Intermediary Identification Number (GIIN).

**FBT (Fringe Benefits Tax)** — Australian tax on non-cash employee benefits. FBT year ends 31 March and is aligned with Macquarie's financial year.

**GST (Goods and Services Tax)** — Australian value-added tax at 10%. Input tax credits are claimable on business purchases. Financial services have reduced input tax credit (RITC) entitlements.

**Hybrid Instrument** — A financial instrument that is treated as debt in one jurisdiction and equity in another, creating potential BEPS mismatches.

**PAYG (Pay As You Go) Withholding** — Employer obligation to withhold tax from employee wages and remit to the ATO.

**Permanent Establishment (PE)** — A fixed place of business in a foreign jurisdiction that creates a taxable presence. PE determinations affect where profits are taxed.

**Tax Consolidation Group** — An Australian tax group in which the head entity (Macquarie Group Limited) and eligible wholly-owned subsidiaries are treated as a single taxpayer.

**Tax Effect Accounting (TEA)** — The process of recognising DTAs and DTLs in the financial statements to match tax expense with accounting profit.

**Tax Provision** — The estimated income tax expense accrued in the financial statements prior to lodgement of the tax return.

**Transfer Pricing (TP)** — Rules governing the pricing of intercompany transactions between related entities in different tax jurisdictions. Macquarie maintains TP documentation aligned with OECD guidelines and local country requirements.

**WHT (Withholding Tax)** — Tax deducted at source on payments (dividends, interest, royalties) made to non-residents. Rates vary by jurisdiction and applicable double tax agreement (DTA).

## Treasury Domain

**ALM (Asset and Liability Management)** — The process of managing the balance sheet to control interest rate risk and liquidity risk, ensuring assets and liabilities are appropriately matched by maturity and rate type.

**Basis Point (bps)** — One hundredth of one percent (0.01%). Used to express interest rate movements and credit spreads.

**Cash Pool** — A structure where balances from multiple bank accounts are aggregated (notionally or physically) to optimise liquidity and minimise external borrowing.

**Collateral** — Assets pledged to secure an obligation. In derivatives, collateral is exchanged daily under CSA (Credit Support Annex) agreements to mitigate counterparty credit risk.

**Counterparty Credit Risk (CCR)** — The risk that a counterparty to a financial contract defaults before settlement. CCR is measured using potential future exposure (PFE) and expected exposure (EE) models.

**CP (Commercial Paper)** — Short-term unsecured debt instruments issued by Macquarie to fund working capital, typically with maturities of up to 270 days.

**CSA (Credit Support Annex)** — A legal agreement specifying the terms under which collateral is posted between parties to a derivatives transaction.

**DV01 (Dollar Value of 01)** — The change in the value of a fixed-income instrument or portfolio for a one-basis-point shift in yield. A key interest rate risk metric.

**ECL (Expected Credit Loss)** — The IFRS 9 credit impairment model requiring recognition of lifetime or 12-month expected credit losses on financial assets.

**FX (Foreign Exchange)** — Currency exchange. Macquarie transacts in multiple currencies; FX risk is managed through hedging with forwards, swaps, and options.

**FX Hedge** — A derivative instrument (forward, option, or swap) used to offset exposure to adverse currency movements.

**HQLA (High-Quality Liquid Assets)** — Assets that can be converted to cash quickly with minimal loss of value; held to meet LCR requirements under APRA APS 210.

**IRS (Interest Rate Swap)** — A derivative contract exchanging fixed-rate interest payments for floating-rate payments (or vice versa) to manage interest rate risk.

**LCR (Liquidity Coverage Ratio)** — APRA-required ratio of HQLA to net cash outflows over a 30-day stress scenario. Minimum 100%.

**MTM (Mark-to-Market)** — Valuation of a financial instrument at current market prices rather than historical cost.

**NSFR (Net Stable Funding Ratio)** — APRA-required ratio ensuring that stable funding sources cover illiquid assets over a one-year horizon. Minimum 100%.

**Netting Agreement** — A legal arrangement allowing offsetting of obligations between two counterparties, reducing gross settlement risk to a net amount.

**NII (Net Interest Income)** — Interest received on assets minus interest paid on liabilities. A primary revenue line for Macquarie Bank.

**REPO (Repurchase Agreement)** — A short-term borrowing arrangement in which securities are sold with an agreement to repurchase them at a specified price and date.

**SWIFT** — Society for Worldwide Interbank Financial Telecommunication. The messaging network used for cross-border payment instructions.

**Term Funding** — Medium to long-term debt raised by Macquarie through bond issuance, covered bonds, or other capital market instruments.

**VaR (Value at Risk)** — A statistical measure of the maximum potential loss of a portfolio over a given time horizon at a specified confidence level (e.g., 1-day 99% VaR).

**XBRL (eXtensible Business Reporting Language)** — Machine-readable format required for regulatory and statutory filings with APRA, ASIC, and some international regulators.

## Cross-Domain Terms

**Business Unit (BU)** — An operating division of Macquarie Group. Primary BUs include Macquarie Asset Management (MAM), Banking and Financial Services (BFS), Commodities and Global Markets (CGM), and Macquarie Capital.

**Data Steward** — The individual responsible for the accuracy, completeness, and governance of a data domain within Finance, Tax, or Treasury.

**Golden Source** — The authoritative, single source of truth for a data element. All downstream consumers must derive from the golden source rather than maintaining independent copies.

**IFRS (International Financial Reporting Standards)** — The accounting standards framework applied by Macquarie Group for consolidated financial reporting.

**Intraday Liquidity** — Liquidity required within the business day to meet settlement obligations in real time.

**LEI (Legal Entity Identifier)** — A 20-character ISO 17442 identifier assigned to each legal entity participating in financial transactions. Required for regulatory reporting.

**PnL Explain** — The decomposition of daily profit and loss into attributable risk factors (delta, gamma, vega, theta, carry, etc.).

**Regulatory Reporting** — Financial and risk data submissions made to regulators (APRA, ASIC, RBA, FCA, SEC, etc.) on prescribed schedules and in prescribed formats.

**SOX (Sarbanes-Oxley)** — US legislation imposing internal control requirements on financial reporting. Macquarie's US-listed entities comply with SOX Section 302 and 404.

**UAM (User Access Management)** — The process of provisioning, reviewing, and revoking access to Finance, Tax, and Treasury systems. Follows least-privilege and quarterly recertification requirements.
